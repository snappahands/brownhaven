ITM Program

"In The Mortgage"

In The Mortgage

What Is ITM?

What It Is

Lets homebuyers roll a Deako pack directly into their mortgage — paying small monthly increments instead of a lump sum at closing.

How It Works

Buyer selects a pack after ratification. Agent approves. A change order is created. Safe Haven installs the smart switches during the "Home is Connected" appointment.

"Roll it into the mortgage."

No upfront cost. No risk to the sale. A closing tool — not a closing obstacle.

Step by Step

The ITM Process

1

At Ratification

Buyer adds a Deako pack. If yes, skip to Step 4.

2

Deako Marketing Email

After ratification, buyer receives an automatic email from Deako and selects their pack.

3

Agent Notified

You receive a notification. You have final say — reply to approve or decline.

4

Change Order + PO

Change order is created, PO is issued, Deako sends to Safe Haven.

5

Installation

Safe Haven installs smart switches at the "Home is Connected" appointment.

Handling Objections

What We Hear & How to Respond

"This will add cost to the home."
Deako packs roll into the mortgage via ITM — buyers pay small monthly increments, not a lump sum. No cost to construction.
"Buyers won't qualify."
Agents approve/reject all requests before anything enters the system. Unqualified buyers can pay cash or finance directly through Deako.
"The home won't appraise."
Smart lighting packs are a non-construction change — they don't affect the appraisal.
"I don't want to risk the sale."
You have full control. One reply to the Deako email removes any buyer who doesn't qualify. Zero risk to your closing.
"I'm reducing the home price and don't want funds used for upgrades."
Deako packs allow Horton to maintain purchase price while still offering a non-construction incentive — a win for both sides.
